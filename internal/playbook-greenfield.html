Greenfield Bank Launch (6-month playbook)

UST Internal 6 months · M0 → M6 Owner: FinX Practice Ops v3.2 · 22 Apr 2026
UST Internal · Do not share externally This playbook contains commercial framing, pricing rationale, and named-client lessons learned. It is for UST FinX practice members only. Do not paste into proposals, share over public channels, or reproduce in client-facing material without explicit sign-off from PracticeOps.

The Greenfield Bank Launch playbook covers the standard 6-month shape we run when a client is launching a new bank or a new digital brand from zero — no legacy core, no incumbent ops surface, no migration. It is the fastest path to first value FinX supports, and the engagement shape we recommend by default for digital subsidiaries and challenger spin-outs.

Overview

Greenfield is the cleanest engagement shape: no legacy parity work, no migration, no shadow-mode comparison. The bank stands up FinX Glue against Thought Machine Vault Core (or another supported core) plus a core set of ecosystem connectors, and goes live with a single product line — typically current accounts plus a card. Subsequent products follow on a 4-8 week cadence.

Most greenfield engagements compress to 6 months because there is no parity gate, no legacy data clean-up, and no operational hand-over. The risk profile is also lower: there is no live customer base to disrupt. The work is mostly forward, and the team can run a single delivery rhythm rather than parallel parity and forward streams.

Phases

Six monthly phases, each with a single primary outcome. Each phase has its own readiness checklist (linked in the sidebar). Phase gates are hard: do not advance to the next phase until the current gate is signed off by the engagement lead and the client sponsor.

Discover
Scope & design
Target operating model, core selection, connector set, regulatory scope.
Setup
Tenancy & foundations
Glue tenancy, environments, identity, observability, change controls.
Build
Onboarding & accounts
Onboarding journey, account opening on Vault Core, IDV+AML wired in.
Build
Cards & payments
Card issuance, faster payments rail, statements, basic ops surface.
Harden
UAT & pen-test
End-to-end UAT, security testing, regulatory walkthroughs, runbooks.
Launch
Pilot & cutover
Friends-and-family pilot, public launch, hyper-care, hand-over to BAU.

Phases overlap by roughly two weeks at each boundary. The gating activities (sign-offs, regulatory readiness, security review) sit at the close of each phase and do not overlap.

Team composition

The default greenfield team is 14 FTE across six functions, plus client-side counterparts in roughly the same ratio. Numbers below are UST-side only.

Reusable artefacts

The following assets are pre-built and should be lifted from the practice library at the start of every greenfield engagement. Do not re-create from scratch; extend in place.

  • GBL-001 Tenancy bootstrap script — provisions Glue tenancy, default RBAC roles, observability, and a sandbox environment in one run.
  • GBL-014 Onboarding journey template — the canonical 6-step onboarding flow pre-wired against Vault Core, Jumio, and ComplyAdvantage.
  • GBL-022 Vault Core configuration baseline — current account product, fee schedule, statement cadence, plus the recommended ledger structure.
  • GBL-031 Glass role pack: greenfield — pre-configured CSR, Compliance, Supervisor, Admin role definitions tuned for a single-product launch.
  • GBL-040 Pen-test scope & vendor brief — pre-approved vendor list, scope template, and the standard remediation SLAs.
  • GBL-052 Hyper-care runbook — week-by-week support shape for the 6 weeks post-launch, including escalation matrix and incident war-room template.

References to past engagements

This playbook is the consolidation of two production runs: Bank A (2024) — a digital subsidiary launch in EMEA, delivered in 5.5 months — and Bank C (2025) — a challenger spin-out in APAC, delivered in 6.5 months including a regional regulatory readiness extension. Both engagements have full retrospectives in Client Engagements → Bank A and Client Engagements → Bank C. Read the retros before you scope a new greenfield: the deltas between Bank A and Bank C show where the playbook is rigid and where it must flex.
