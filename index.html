The Problem

Modernisation has been broken for too long.

Bank technology leaders are asked to deliver continuous innovation on platforms designed for an era of episodic change. The two prevailing answers — replace the core, or bolt on point solutions — both fail in predictable ways. Programmes overrun. Architectures fragment. Risk accumulates faster than value is delivered.

Big-bang transformation

Multi-year core replacements promise a clean slate but routinely overrun by years and over-budget by hundreds of millions. The longer the programme runs, the further the destination drifts from the bank that commissioned it. Few finish. Fewer succeed.

Point solutions

The opposite extreme — a fintech for every gap — produces short-term wins and long-term entropy. Each integration ages independently, contracts proliferate, the canonical model fragments, and operations inherit a permanent integration tax that grows year over year.

The FinX Approach

A different model. Continuous evolution, not episodic transformation.

FinX is not a product line. It is a delivery model: a BIAN-aligned orchestration layer, an iterative engagement pattern, and a coexistence strategy that keeps the existing core productive while new capability is composed around it.

Architecture

BIAN-aligned by design

Every API, every service domain, every canonical model maps to BIAN. The architecture is not opinion — it is industry standard. Banks gain interoperability, vendor optionality, and a shared language with their ecosystem partners.

Delivery

Iterative value, every quarter

Engagements are scoped to deliver measurable business outcomes in 6–9 month cycles, not multi-year programmes. The bank sees value early, learns continuously, and compounds capability with each release.

Coexistence

Managed legacy, not ripped legacy

The existing core stays in place and stays productive. Glue mediates between legacy and new — exposing modern APIs over old systems while gradually migrating service domains as the business case justifies.

The Platform

FinX Glue at the centre.
Everything else composable around it.

Glue is the orchestration layer: BIAN-aligned APIs, a canonical data model, a workflow engine, and a curated set of ecosystem connectors. Glass is the operations surface above it. Vault is the modern core beneath it. The bank's existing systems, fintech partners, and data platforms compose into a single coherent runtime.

Modernization Paths

Choose how to start. Evolve from there.

There is no single right answer. Banks start where the business case is clearest and expand from there. Glue and the canonical model are the constants — the path through them is the bank's choice.

Path 01

Greenfield

Stand up a new digital brand or subsidiary on Glue + Vault from day one. Fastest path to a clean architecture without legacy migration risk.

Time to first value: 6 months

Path 02

Coexistence

Wrap the existing core with Glue to expose modern APIs and start composing new journeys without disturbing the systems of record.

Time to first value: 4–6 months

Path 03

Progressive

Migrate service domain by service domain. Party first, then Accounts, then Payments — with the canonical model holding the seam.

Time to first value: 9 months · per domain

Path 04

Full Modernization

Multi-year programme to retire the legacy core entirely, sequenced through Coexistence and Progressive milestones rather than one big-bang event.

Time to full cutover: 24–36 months
