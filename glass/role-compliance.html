Compliance Officer

7 common tasks 3 modules Regulated

Compliance Officers own the regulatory posture of every customer the bank onboards or services. In Glass, that means working through KYC outcomes, AML alerts, sanctions hits, and four-eyes approvals from a single task queue — with the underlying customer record, document evidence, and connector findings all one click away.

What this role does

A Compliance Officer in Glass spends most of the day in two places: the Task Management queue (where KYC reviews and AML alerts arrive) and the Customer & Account Management module (where the underlying customer record lives). The Glue platform feeds both — IDV outcomes from Jumio, screening results from ComplyAdvantage, and lifecycle states from Party Lifecycle Management all surface as actionable tasks rather than raw events.

The role is regulated. Every decision is auditable, every approval has a four-eyes counterpart, and every override emits a structured event to the bank's evidence store.

Common tasks

The seven tasks below cover roughly 90% of the working day. Click into any task for a step-by-step walkthrough.

Review KYC results for a new customer
Inspect IDV findings from Jumio, AML screening from ComplyAdvantage, and approve or escalate. ~5 min.
→
Approve a high-risk customer
Two-step approval workflow with senior compliance sign-off. Required for any customer scored above the bank's risk threshold.
→
Handle an AML alert
Triage transaction-monitoring alerts. Mark as false positive, escalate to a SAR, or freeze the account in line with policy.
→
Run sanctions screening
Ad-hoc screening for an existing customer or counterparty. Results stream back from ComplyAdvantage in under a second.
→
Provide four-eyes approval
Second-line review of a peer's decision. Required for high-value account changes, manual KYC overrides, and SAR submissions.
→
Conduct a periodic review
Annual or risk-triggered KYC refresh. Glass surfaces customers due for review and pre-fills the diff against the last attested record.
→
File a Suspicious Activity Report
Generate the regulator-ready SAR document from the case file and submit through the configured reporting channel.
→

Required permissions

The default compliance-officer role bundles the permissions below. Banks can extend or restrict the bundle from the Admin module without affecting other roles.

Permission Scope Notes
kyc.review approve Approve, reject, or escalate KYC outcomes from new onboardings and periodic reviews.
aml.alert.handle write Triage and resolve AML alerts. Cannot delete alerts; only mark as resolved or escalated.
sanctions.screen write Initiate ad-hoc sanctions screening against any party or counterparty.
customer.read read Read full customer record including documents and connector evidence. Required for review work.
customer.lifecycle.write write Update party lifecycle status (suspend, reactivate, close) within the compliance-allowed transitions.
approval.foureyes approve Act as the second pair of eyes on a peer's decision. Cannot self-approve own decisions.
sar.submit write Generate and submit Suspicious Activity Reports to the configured reporting endpoint.

Module access

The Compliance Officer role sees three Glass modules in the primary navigation. Other modules remain hidden unless an admin grants additional roles.
