Reviewing KYC results for a new customer

5 min read Module: Customer & Account Mgmt Role: Compliance Officer

When a new customer completes the digital onboarding flow, Glue runs IDV through Jumio and AML screening through ComplyAdvantage in parallel. The combined outcome lands in your Compliance task queue as a KYC Review task. This walkthrough takes you from picking up the task to lifting the customer to active.

Overview

A KYC review takes most officers between three and seven minutes when the underlying signals are clean. The longer cases — manual document checks, partial matches on PEP lists, address-mismatch flags — have their own escalation paths covered in Approve a high-risk customer. Here we focus on the standard happy-path review.

Step-by-step

  1. Open the Task Management console

    From the Glass left navigation, choose Task Management → My queue. KYC reviews are tagged kyc.review and ordered by SLA expiry. Click the task to open it; the customer record opens in the right pane while the task header stays pinned at the top.

    glass.ustfinx.com / tasks / my-queue
    cust_8a2f · Asha Mehta · KYC ReviewSLA 2h 14m
    cust_7b3e · Daniel Roy · KYC ReviewSLA 5h 02m
    cust_4c1d · Priya Iyer · AML AlertBreached

  2. Review the customer profile and connector findings

    The right pane shows the consolidated customer record. Cross-check the four panels in order: Identity (name, DOB, address as submitted vs. as verified by Jumio), Document (the uploaded ID with Jumio's authenticity score), Sanctions & PEP (ComplyAdvantage match list, with confidence scores), and Risk score (the bank's policy-derived composite). Hover any field to see the source connector and timestamp.

    glass.ustfinx.com / customers / cust_8a2f / kyc
    Identity match (Jumio)98% · Pass
    Document authenticityGenuine · Pass
    Sanctions screening (ComplyAdvantage)No match
    PEP screeningNo match
    Adverse media1 weak match · Review
    Composite risk score22 / 100 · Low

  3. Make your decision

    The Decision bar at the bottom of the task offers three options. Pick one and add a reason note (mandatory). On confirm, Glass writes the decision event to the audit store and Glue updates the party lifecycle on the underlying core.

    • APPROVE Approve and lift to active. Sends a PATCH /v1/parties/{id}/lifecycle with status active. The customer is notified and can transact within seconds.
    • ESCALATE Escalate to senior compliance. Routes the task to the senior queue with your notes attached. Use this for any weak adverse-media match or composite score above 60.
    • REJECT Reject and close. Sends a PATCH /v1/parties/{id}/lifecycle with status rejected. A reason note is required and is surfaced verbatim in the customer's regulator-facing audit trail.
Permission required You need the kyc.review permission to action this task and customer.lifecycle.write to complete the lift to active. Both are bundled in the default compliance-officer role. Approvals above the bank's risk threshold additionally require an approval.foureyes sign-off from a peer — Glass routes that automatically.
